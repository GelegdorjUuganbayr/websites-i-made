Here's a timeline of S.N.Goenka's life

  • 1924: January 30

    Satya Narayana Goenka is born in Mandalay, Burma (now Myanmar) to a Marwari family of Indian origin.

  • 1955

    Goenka begins suffering from severe migraines and, after seeking medical relief, meets Sayagyi U Ba Khin, a Vipassana teacher, and begins his meditation practice under his guidance.

  • 1969

    After 14 years of training, Goenka is authorized by Sayagyi U Ba Khin to teach Vipassana meditation. He moves to India and leaves his business.

  • 1976

    Goenka opens the Dhamma Giri Meditation Centre in Igatpuri, Maharashtra, India, becoming a key center for Vipassana practice.

  • 1982

    Goenka begins training assistant teachers to help expand the teaching of Vipassana meditation.

  • 1985

    Goenka establishes the Vipassana Research Institute at Dhamma Giri to investigate and publish literature on Vipassana and its effects.

  • 1988

    Goenka’s Vipassana courses expand globally, with thousands of Westerners attending his 10-day meditation courses.

  • 2000

    Goenka lays the foundation for the Global Vipassana Pagoda near Mumbai, which would become the largest pillar-less stone dome in the world. It opens in 2009.

  • 2002

    Goenka embarks on a Meditation Now Tour across North America, sharing his teachings with a wider audience.

  • 2012

    Goenka is awarded the Padma Bhushan, the third-highest civilian honor in India, for his distinguished service in spreading Vipassana meditation.

  • 2013: September 29

    Goenka passes away at the age of 89 at his home in Mumbai. He is survived by his wife Elaichi Devi Goenka and six sons.

  • Posthumous Legacy

    Goenka’s teachings continue to be practiced at 380 Vipassana centers in 94 countries. His recordings continue to guide students, and his influence is felt worldwide in the fields of meditation and mindfulness.
